Myths vs Facts

The claims you've probably heard — checked

Tap or click a card to flip it and see the fact behind each myth.

Myth

"It's natural, so it's basically harmless."

Tap to see the fact →
Fact

Plenty of natural things carry real risk — smoke inhalation and psychoactive effects don't stop being real just because the source is a plant.

Myth

"Weed isn't addictive."

Tap to see the fact →
Fact

Regular use can lead to real dependency (Cannabis Use Disorder) — needing more for the same effect, and difficulty stopping even when you want to.

Myth

"It won't affect my studies or work."

Tap to see the fact →
Fact

Regular use is linked to reduced memory, concentration, and follow-through — effects that can outlast the high itself, especially with frequent use.

Myth

"It's basically legal in India now."

Tap to see the fact →
Fact

Cannabis (ganja/weed) remains illegal to possess, grow, or sell under the NDPS Act, 1985, almost everywhere in India. See The Law for details.

Myth

"You can't get hooked if you only smoke occasionally."

Tap to see the fact →
Fact

Usage patterns tend to escalate over time for some people, and risk isn't zero at any frequency — "occasional" is often how regular use starts.

Myth

"It's fine to drive if I only had a little."

Tap to see the fact →
Fact

Even small amounts measurably slow reaction time and impair coordination — driving under the influence is a real, elevated accident risk, not a myth.
